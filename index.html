What are the 12 values?

Every value is paired to its opposite so as to form 6 independent axes:

Level Those with higher Level scores believe that productive assets deserve to be placed under collective stewardship. They tend to support worker self-management, communal land trusts, and social access to capital. Ownership Strata Those with higher Strata scores believe that hierarchy in the economy organizes merit and incentives ideally. They tend to support private workplace control, claims-based property, and class systems. Command Those with higher Command scores believe that tight coordination is needed throughout supply chains. They tend to support price controls, strategic subsidies, and industry quotas. Production Demand Those with higher Demand scores believe that producers should adapt fluidly to consumption signals. They tend to support market pricing, entrepreneurial initiative, and laissez-faire. Unity Those with higher Unity scores believe that governance requires a single unambiguous chain of command. They tend to support sturdy leadership, central management, and autocracy. Structure Autonomy Those with higher Autonomy scores believe that social order must emerge deliberately from the bottom-up. They tend to support plural interests, self-organization, and anarchy. Volition Those with higher Volition scores believe that personal commitments are best defined without coercion. They tend to support individualism, free expression, and bodily autonomy. Legality Obligation Those with higher Obligation scores believe that being a member of society comes with inherent civic duties. They tend to support intrinsic social bonds, cohesive conformity, and paternalism. Inclusion Those with higher Inclusion scores believe that [Broad belief]. They tend to support [Policy #1], [Policy #2], and [Policy #3]. Identity Supremacy Those with higher Supremacy scores believe that [Broad belief]. They tend to support [Policy #1], [Policy #2], and [Policy #3]. Sanctity Those with higher Sanctity scores believe that time-tested norms encode irreducible wisdom towards flourishing. They tend to support traditional aesthetics, religious fundamentalism, and perennial virtues. Culture Novelty Those with higher Novelty scores believe that cultural development demands perpetual adaptation to arising contexts. They tend to support artistic rejuvenation, metaphysical reassessment, and moral transvaluation.
